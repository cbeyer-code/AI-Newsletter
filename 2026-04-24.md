## Models & Research

OpenAi also released their new image model, which is dominating all benchmarks and showed a massive jump in capabilities, especially when it comes to the generation of text.  
It might slowly enter the realm where useful infographics for scientific articles or reports can be generated with very few hallucinations. I recommend looking at examples by Ethan Mollick.  
<https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-images-2-0/>  
<https://x.com/emollick/status/2046665274535854146>  
OpenAI introduced Chronicles for Codex, which is a system that creates memory files from screenshots, so that it is easier to communicate with your agent. The idea is that you can say: ‘  
Fix this error.’ and Codex will then be able to figure out what you mean by ‘this’.  
I find the idea of screenshots taken by an AI quite creepy, though, even if they claim that   
they are stored and processed only locally.   
<https://developers.openai.com/codex/memories/chronicle>  
  
Kimi K2.6 was released, which is now topping the open-source leaderboards. It supports long-horizon tasks and was specifically tuned for software engineering. While it overperforms on benchmarks (often better than certain frontier models), it is still a notable advancement overall, even if this does not translate as well into daily performance.   
<https://www.kimi.com/blog/kimi-k2-6>  
<https://x.com/emollick/status/2046430301593751797>  
  
Yann LeCun has long argued that LLMs are poor at understanding reality and forming abstractions, leading them to rely a lot on memorization. Therefore, he worked on a different machine learning paradigm, which is called JEPA (Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture), which is supposed to actually help models understand reality and ground predictions in ‘laws of physics’, which the models abstract from observed training data. JEPA had multiple open research problems that hindered the models from actually learning anything useful, but now a paper has been released that solved most of them and might make this a viable competitor, if not the new baseline, especially for visual understanding and prediction.   
<https://x.com/HowToAI_/status/2046254937559237012>  
<https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.19312>  
  
In this clip, Dr. Fei-Fei-Li explains why learning household tasks, like emptying a dishwasher, is really hard for Robots. It is mostly because of the complexity of the tasks and the multiple steps that are needed, which make it very hard to steer the models using reinforcement learning.  
<https://x.com/rohanpaul_ai/status/2045931489758683175>

## Politics & Society & Safety

Journalist Rachel Metz found out that Anthropic’s most powerful model, Mythos, has been accessed by people who were not allowed to do so. That’s the model that found security vulnerabilities in all the major software, which could be exploited by bad actors.   
<https://x.com/rachelmetz/status/2046710829530370336>  
<https://t.co/eBqNtS7bRJ>  
Mythos is also being used by the NSA, even though Anthropic was designated a supply-chain risk. Given the Snowden files, some might say the NSA is an actor that should be kept away from these kind of models, as they hoarded exploits in order to infiltrate computer systems at will.
