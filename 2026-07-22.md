## Models

It was really a big week for open-source models, and while China set the tone by pretty much catching up to American frontier labs, the US and Germany also had a great week.  
  
Thinking Machines, which has a lot of former OpenAI employees, released a multi-model open-weight model called Inkling that is especially good with audio and has almost a trillion parameters.  
<https://thinkingmachines.ai/news/introducing-inkling/>  
  
A team from Germany managed to release Soofi S, which is a 30B mixture-of-experts model aimed at the European market, which reaches unparalleled detail in how it was trained.  
All datasets and the whole training procedure are transparent, which is fantastic. It also performs just short of QWEN, and the lead author said that they are currently also training a large version of the model.   
<https://www.soofi.info/>  
<https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.09424>  
  
OpenAI uses an internal model called RED for auto red teaming their models. Red teaming has the goal is to circumvent the AI’s safety mechanisms so that it helps with unwanted behaviour like helping to create (cyber) weapons or drugs. This is also an early form of self-improvement as an AI is helping to produce a better AI that is less susceptible to prompt-injections.  
<https://openai.com/index/unlocking-self-improvement-gpt-red/>  
  
OpenAI also released a hardware gadget for agentic coding which lets you quickly change between workflows, prompt using audio, and customize some other buttons.  
<https://overclock3d.net/news/input_devices/openai-hardware-has-arrived-and-im-not-impressed/>  
I read this interesting article about how agentic coding has changed the role of developers and how it can now be more exhausting: 'If you're feeling like your work is simultaneously more productive and less satisfying, you're not broken. The feedback loop is broken. And I think we need to start treating that as an engineering problem in its own right, not a personal failure.'  
<https://pydantic.dev/articles/the-human-in-the-loop-is-tired>  
  
It seems that GPT5.6 has reached an IQ of 136, which puts it in the genius class, outperforming over 99% of humans. This is just more evidence to me that a sudden drop in performance gains seems unlikely.  
<https://www.htx.com/news/gpt-56s-iq-breaks-130-genius-threshold-for-the-first-time-ou-jiAuG6wI/>  
Especially, as the model is only one component in an AI-based workflow. Another extremely important one seems to be the harness, so the tools and framework that guide and use your AI. Here, some people claim that they can achieve almost 99% on the ARC-AGI 3 benchmark (unknown games with growing complexity) with their harness that tries to make an LLM think like a physicist: ‘There is only one way to make progress: the physicist’s way. The agent must act while its model of the game is still provisional, forming hypotheses about what the grid represents, how actions change it, and what counts as success, then revising both its model and its plan as new observations arrive.’  
<https://schema-harness.github.io/>  
<https://arcprize.org/scorecards/5e258a92-bf42-4dca-b013-eafbbb6baea6>  
<https://x.com/HavenFeng/status/2077772576571666750>

## Society and Safety

Unfortunately, a new level of AI-based warfare has been reached with fully autonomous killer drones being employed by Ukraine in the defence of their homeland. This is an ethical nightmare, especially if you imagine that some disturbed kids can vibe-code such weapons   
using open models in their basement.   
The incident reported here is already two years old, but I also saw recent footage on Twitter.  
<https://www.newscientist.com/article/2529849-fully-autonomous-drones-have-killed-human-soldiers-for-the-first-time/>  
The involvement of frontier labs in the development of AI weapon systems is for many people of great concern, including employees at Google DeepMind.  
<https://www.businessinsider.com/google-deepmind-ai-researcher-resign-military-contract-pentagon-2026-7>  
  
Here is a great talk by a philosopher about AI and who we talk to when we engage with a chatbot. He says that all requirements for consciousness except carbon-based biology are in the process of being fulfilled. Current models already have quasi beliefs and desires. For a lot of purposes, including safety, quasi beliefs are as relevant as real beliefs. He gives jokingly the example: ‘Doesn't help if AI doesn't really want to kill us all if it behaves as if it does want to kill us all.’
