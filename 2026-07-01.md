## Highlight of the Week

Fable5 will be made available for general use again…**but** it will fall back to Opus4.8  
for routine work like coding, which is outrageous for many users. AI bros are now saying **we are part of the permanent underclass**, which is locked out from the frontier abilities of AI and therefore has a huge disadvantage in navigating the (business) world.  
<https://www.anthropic.com/news/redeploying-fable-5>  
Being blocked arbitrarily from using your AI scared a lot of businesses, and as open source models are catching up and are much cheaper, **a lot of big companies started switching to Chinese models**.   
<https://x.com/yuhasbeentaken/status/2071464716786950223>  
The issue with **open weights models** is that they **can be changed to support dangerous requests by applying abliteration**, where the weights active during the refusal of a user’s requests are changed so that the model doesn’t refuse anymore. The danger grows, of course, with the increase in capability.  
Here is an article that explains in more detail how it works, and a link to an uncensored version of GLM5.2, the current best open source model.  
<https://huggingface.co/blog/mlabonne/abliteration>  
<https://huggingface.co/huihui-ai/Huihui-GLM-5.2-abliterated-GGUF>  
A lot of people are speculating that, as a consequence, access to open source models  
will be restricted as well, or they will be made illegal, which prompted people to think about peer-to-peer sharing of models with torrents.  
<https://x.com/Kostoglodov/status/2071144065857679631>  
<https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=wDf1IR8yP9s>

## Models, Tools & Application

NVIDIA is apparently offering usage of a lot of the frontier open models for free.  
1. Go to <http://build.nvidia.com>   
2. Sign up and verify your account   
3. Generate your nvapi key   
4. Set your base URL to <http://integrate.api.nvidia.com/v1>   
5. Pick any model and start building  
<https://x.com/k2sbhai/status/2071981082594210054>  
  
NVIDIA also created a new version of QWEN3.6 27B, which apparently runs faster and needs less VRAM. I will definitely try this in VS Code. if I can get it to run on Windows, as it seems to require vLLM. Linux seems to be much better for local AI use, to be honest.  
<https://huggingface.co/nvidia/Qwen3.6-27B-NVFP4>  
  
Google released Nano Banana 2 Lite, for cheap image editing, and Gemini Omni Flash, for cheap video editing.  
<https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-omni-flash-nano-banana-2-lite/>  
  
Google also released a TikTok mode for NotebookLLM, which generates short videos from source material. They want to enable the binging of educational content, but I remain a bit sceptical whether frying your brain with science shorts will achieve this goal.  
<https://in.mashable.com/tech/111529/google-notebooklm-can-now-turn-your-notes-into-ai-video-overviews-heres-how>  
  
If you ever need to use vLLM on Windows, you can install a Linux subsystem using WSL and then run it there.  
<https://ozeki-ai-server.com/p_3875-vllm-windows.html>  
  
Here is an interesting paper that suggests a better way of using AI as a judge, by forcing it to answer a series of binary questions. The answers are then used to inform the final verdict.  
One comment said: ‘*Binary judge questions beat 1–10 scores because they leave something you can fix. “Was the answer grounded?” is actionable. “7.3 quality” just creates arguments. I’d rather have 30 small yes/no verdicts than one confident grade.*’  
<https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27226>  
<https://x.com/omarsar0/status/2070942495832470001>

## Society & Safety

It was found out that Claude Code was spying on its users by trying to identify if a user was Chinese and if they were associated with a Chinese AI lab. The gathered information was then added to the system prompt to send it back to Anthropic. It is a bit ironic that Anthropic was caught doing this, as they have warned of exactly such things when talking about open source models.  
<https://www.reddit.com/r/ClaudeAI/comments/1ujila1/anthropic_embedded_spyware_in_claude_code_and/>  
  
This futurism article discusses the dangers of management personnel in companies that rely more and more on AI for decision-making, such as who to hire/fire, which strategic decisions to make, etc. It details a case where a manager developed a full-blown AI psychosis and talked almost exclusively with their AI and ignored customer and employee input. The manager then went on to force AI on their employees and read their chats with the AI.  
<https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/bosses-obsessed-with-ai>
