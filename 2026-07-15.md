## Highlights of the Week

GPT5.6 is the first model to make noticeable progress on one of my favourite benchmarks: ARC-AGI 3.  
The benchmark tests whether AI can pick up rules by playing games completely without instructions, which the AI has to transfer to new levels as the complexity grows level by level.  
While the overall score of 7.8% is still low and far below human levels (>90%), it is still a huge jump because GPT5.5 scored only 0.4%.  
<https://arcprize.org/leaderboard>  
To me, it seems that we have hit another milestone in terms of general ability, which will only get better. There are demos where users tasked GPT5.6 to build a cannon or bat in Blender and to record a video of how it did it, and it just works now.

## Society & Safety

Meta is involved in multiple scandals right now (again), as their newest image model, which they had introduced on Instagram, extracted user data by default, and users had to actively opt out of their data being scraped. This created a big backlash, forcing them to reverse the rollout.  
<https://www.reuters.com/technology/meta-discontinues-ai-image-feature-days-after-launch-2026-07-10/>  
  
Furthermore, employees claim that Meta used AI to identify workers with potential (mental) health problems so they can be fired in advance, which has huge ethical implications.  
One user on Twitter had already suspected that news like ‘AI can identify cancer 5 years ahead of doctors’ will actually be weaponized against workers so that companies can fire vulnerable people in advance instead of providing better health care and early treatment.  
<https://www.reuters.com/world/meta-used-ai-target-workers-with-medical-conditions-layoffs-former-employees-2026-07-14/>  
<https://x.com/jjellisart/status/2077179328672989535>

The authors of AI2027, one of my favourite AI progress predictions, initially predicted that AI doom is very likely when we uncontrollably race towards superintelligence.  
They have now released a potential plan towards safe super intelligence called AI2040 plan A, which focuses on international cooperation, shared research, control, and monitoring of compute. I highly recommend reading it and advocating for such a scenario with relevant decision makers.  
<https://ai-2040.com/?r=tw&choices=plan-a-root>  
At the same time, people are already demonstrating in the USA for a stop of the AI race and for a pause until the dangers and capabilities of current models are better understood.  
<https://www.youtube.com/shorts/4t6Pzs8z280>
