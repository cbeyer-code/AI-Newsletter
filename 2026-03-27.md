## Highlight of the Week

DeepSeek seems to have developed a meaningful addition to the transformer architecture, which basically adds a hash-based lookup table based on n-grams that enables the storage of knowledge. They call it conditional memory, and this video explains it wonderfully, but the creators say that the paper is brilliantly written, and therefore, I would recommend reading it as preparation for your master's thesis. This also hints that the release of the new deepseek model is imminent, which is rumored to have a trillion parameters.  
<https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07372v1>  
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xUlX6jvwVfM>

## Performance and Capabilities

The ARC-AGI 3 benchmark was released, where AI-agents have to play unknown games without instructions, which tests whether they can:  
•Explore the environment • Form hypotheses • Execute a plan • Learn and adapt  
Gemini 3.1pro performs well on some but badly on others, and overall, the gap between humans and AIs is still quite large. I would strongly encourage you to try some of the games :-)

## Miscellaneous

If you want to run LLMs locally without a big hassle, for instance, to work on documents that contain private information, I can recommend LM-Studio, which is very easy to install and lets you download open-source LLMs and run them just like ChatGPT with multiple chats and copyable code blocks, etc. I tried it on Windows, but it should also work on Linux and Mac.  
It is also amazing to see the whole internal thinking process before the final answer is generated.  
<https://lmstudio.ai/>

Google Docs allows continuous scanning of documents with duplicate detection, which can be incredibly useful when digitizing your learning materials and personal notes.  
<https://x.com/minchoi/status/2035101387298611651>

Sakana.AI paper on fully automated AI research was published in Nature as an open-access article. This article shows new scaling laws that imply that, as foundation models get better, automated research does as well.
