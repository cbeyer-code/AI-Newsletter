## Society

The president of Ukraine claims that they have developed weapon systems for ground operations that are completely controlled remotely, and that they managed to capture a position without any humans being on site. He says this is great because it saves  
Ukrainian soldiers' lives, but ethically, such technology is questionable, especially when it is clear that such systems will be run fully autonomously by AI in the future.  
Many people fear that making killing risk-free will encourage using violence as a political means. The technology might save lives now and help them regain their territories and freedom, which would be great, but what happens when Russia or other countries catch up and then send killer robots into Ukraine or other countries?   
This is why a lot of people ask for international treaties to govern the use of killer robots, especially when they are fully autonomous.  
<https://www.politico.eu/article/volodymyr-zelenskyy-robotic-systems-russia-army-positions-ukraine/>  
<https://x.com/KaterynaLis/status/2043827043863863404>  
<https://www.hrw.org/news/2025/05/21/un-start-talks-treaty-ban-killer-robots>

There is a new paper that talks about AI and job replacement, where the authors argue that   
companies will be forced to replace humans because of competitive pressure, even if it shows that the replaced humans will suffer financially and therefore can purchase fewer goods.  
This will lead to a spiral where better models replace more and more people, leading to a breakdown of established capitalist structures. It's a prisoner's dilemma where the companies cannot stop replacement even if they know that it will lead to an overall bad outcome for society and themselves. The authors also explored different political measures, like universal basic income, and found that the only viable option seems to be to tax the companies for their AI use.   
<https://www.reddit.com/r/ArtificialInteligence/comments/1sjv0wo/interesting_paper_on_ai_layoffs_and_why_firms_may/>  
<https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20617>
