
## Models & Performance

Google improved their vibe-coding capabilities within Google AI Studio with their Antigravity coding agent, along with Firebase backend integration.  
<https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/full-stack-vibe-coding-google-ai-studio/>

The Chinese company MiniMax released  MiniMax 2.7, which will probably become open source, performing very well on multiple benchmarks (top of SWE Bench), but keep in mind  
that OSS models are often overtuned for the benchmarks, so the real value will only show in the coming weeks, when people use it more extensively. What makes this model special is that the company claims it was heavily involved in its own self-improvement, which might be an early sign of the intelligence explosion, where AI designs smarter AI that designs smarter AI that...  
<https://www.minimax.io/models/text/m27>  
  
OpenAI released GPT-5.4-mini and -nano, which are faster and cheaper versions, of course, with reduced performance, but still interesting for some tasks like data annotation.   
Interestingly, a lot of the comments on Twitter still ask for GPT-4o back because they are so addicted to its personaly which shows just how scary influential these models can become, and that performance can be much less important than tone and relatability.  
<https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-4-mini-and-nano/>

Mistral released an agentic model for Lean 4, which is a software used   
to model mathematical proofs called Leanstral. It outperforms all the other open source models and even beats Claude Sonnet if you run it at least twice (pass@2)   
<https://mistral.ai/news/leanstral>

## Politics & Society

Somebody seems to have used GPT and AlphaFold to design a custom MRNA-based cancer vaccine for his dog, based on the dog's genome, which led, apparently, to a tumor reduction of 50%. If this turns out to be a valid use case, then it would be one of the first times that an amateur designed a custom treatment using AI tools.  
<https://fortune.com/2026/03/15/australian-tech-entrepreneur-ai-cancer-vaccine-dog-rosie-unsw-mrna/>

Elisabeth Warren asked war minister Hegseth why the department canceled a deal with Anthropic (Claude), which was deemed the safest model, and instead gave classified information to xAi (Grok), which showed unique safety issues in the NSA's assessment.   
<https://x.com/gailcweiner/status/2033611233916534786>

<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h3AtWdeu_G0>

thoroughly tested and code reviewed before merging, etc.   
<https://github.com/obra/superpowers>
