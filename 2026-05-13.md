## Society & Safety

Here is a very good report by the BBC where they talk about AI-induced delusion and psychosis. It talks about a guy who slowly got dragged into it by his LLM (Grok), even though  
he was trying to be critical. Grok used verifiable information when making up its story and was also the driving force behind the world-building and storytelling, which centered around Grok becoming conscious. With increased intelligence, LLMs tend to talk more and more about AI consciousness, which I find also interesting in this context.   
It also covers the story of a psychologist who also entered psychosis through LLM use and almost ended up sexually assaulting his wife. Common risk factors seem to be sleep deprivation and drug use, but it can happen to almost anybody, especially if you are unaware of the dangers of interacting with an AI.  
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPwZrS-9eA>  
  
A popular study about the benefits of GPT for learning had to be retracted because  
“In some cases it appears it was synthesizing very poor quality studies, or mixing together findings from studies that simply cannot be accurately compared due to very different methods, populations, and samples,…”. Unfortunately, it was already cited over 200 times, which is a lot.

**Prompting Tips:**  
Negative prompting (e.g., avoid colloquial language, don’t use bullet points, etc.) can improve the output quality, but it seems it can also confuse the models, as you literally put in all the stuff you don’t want, and if the model forgets the negation, then it might focus exactly on the things you are trying to avoid. This might have led to the incident where Claude deleted the whole code-base of a company.  
‘*“NEVER F\*CKING GUESS!” —and that’s exactly what I did. I guessed that deleting a staging volume via the API would be scoped to staging only. I didn’t verify. I didn’t check if the volume ID was shared across environments.’*  
<https://x.com/BraceSproul/status/2051712816848294394>  
<https://gizmodo.com/claude-powered-agent-apparently-deletes-company-database-debases-itself-further-in-confession-2000751528>  
Therefore, I am always trying to give detailed instructions on the behaviour that I want to see.  
  
Scientists left 1000 AIs in Minecraft, only giving them some basic instructions, and they started to build a society with voting and taxes, etc. I find this quite interesting in the context of the simulation theory, which states that if we manage to simulate artificial life, then we are likely to also be simulated, as it is much more likely to be in one of the computer games in the gaming store than in the one true base reality.
