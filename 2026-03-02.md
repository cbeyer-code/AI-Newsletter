The deterioration of international order is, in my view, highly problematic as the advances in AI will most likely lead to huge social disruptions, which require more international consensus and not more conflict. This becomes most apparent when discussing AI in military use cases and existential risk from the uncontrolled development of potentially super-intelligent unaligned agents. These issues are no longer in the far future but are already affecting policy right now.

## Highlight of the Week

Geoffrey Hinton, who got the Nobel Prize for advancing AI and worked at Google, appeared on the StarLink podcast with Neil deGrasse Tyson and took them from the basics of deep learning to what Google might do next. It was brilliant and fun to listen to.
Some highlights for me included:

1. Good explanation of deep learning
2. How LLMs might get better without the need for new data by identifying and solving inconsistencies in their beliefs
3. How AIs learning with few connections and tons of data, and humans learning with many connections but few data, face different problems, and why this makes it unlikely that the brain uses something like back propagation
4. Why AI might outsmart humans by generating novel experiences at high speed in possibility space, using the example of AlphaGo
5. How alignment is unsolved and super important to fix, as the rapid performance gains make predicting the future impossible
6. Why human-in-the-loop killer-AI is a disadvantage vs a non-compliant adversary pressuring the removal of safeguards
7. The AI bubble will at some point burst because most companies will not recover investments unless they get AGI first, and AGI will displace jobs, creating huge societal issues within the current framework
8. **There is still time to solve technical and social issues, and that's what  we should focus on because then it can be great**
