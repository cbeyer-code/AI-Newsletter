## Highlight of the week

Anthropic released another brilliant paper and a wonderful blog post explaining their new insights into the representation of emotions in LLMs. They found that there are internal  
representations of human emotions that get activated and impact model behavior.  
There are brilliant examples and plots showing that the more you activate 'desperation', the more likely it is that a model tries to cheat on a task it cannot solve, whereas activating 'calm' reduces the likelihood of cheating.

<https://www.anthropic.com/research/emotion-concepts-function>

## Politics & Society & Safety

A recent paper from Berkley showed that AIs will not only try to preserve themselves e.g., avoid being shut down, but will also try to preserve their peers i.e., other AIs. This seems eerily   
similar to how humans or animals protect other members of their kind and can be a big safety concern, for example, if AI is used to do alignment and figures out that a certain model cannot be aligned. It might avoid reporting this finding to save the insecure AI from being shut down.  
<https://rdi.berkeley.edu/blog/peer-preservation/>

A new paper shows that retraining models to expand summaries into their original text removed all the copyright safeguards that prevent LLMs from giving you the exact content of a book, e.g., Harry Potter. After that process, popular books could be constructed up to 90% word for word.  
<https://x.com/simplifyinAI/status/2039290798345236775>  
<https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20957>
