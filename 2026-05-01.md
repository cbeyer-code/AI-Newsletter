## Models & Benchmarks

Mistral released  Mistral Medium 3.5, a new vision reasoning model, which shows competitive performance in benchmarks against models up to 7 times its size.  
‘Mistral Medium 3.5 is our first flagship merged model, available in public preview. It is a dense 128B model with a 256k context window, handling instruction-following, reasoning, and coding in a single set of weights. It performs strongly in real-world use, with self-hosting possible on as few as four GPUs.’  
<https://x.com/UnslothAI/status/2049511248623256017>  
<https://mistral.ai/news/vibe-remote-agents-mistral-medium-3-5>

DeepSeek is actually not the best open-source model CURRENTLY (Kimi 2.6 seems best), but its architecture makes it a very strong contender for upgrades and fine-tuning.   
It is also extremely cheap and doesn’t require NVIDIA GPUs to be trained and deployed,  
which could threaten the economic dominance of Western models and is apparently a big reason why NVIDIA will invest 26 billion Dollars into building open-source models themselves.  
<https://x.com/MatthewBerman/status/2047843625510609373>  
<https://www.wired.com/story/nvidia-investing-26-billion-open-source-models/>  
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eV3lAY77IpU>  
  
Amongst all the hype, I want to point to benchmarks where frontier models perform very poorly.   
Here is ARC-AGI 3, where AIs have to learn how to play simple games without instructions.  
It is extremely fun, and I encourage you to try a game. Humans reach around 80% on this benchmark, and current models reach less than 1%.  
<https://arcprize.org/leaderboard>  
Try games here:   
<https://arcprize.org/tasks/ls20>  
The reasons why current models like Opus 4.7 and GPT 5.5 fail are discussed here and mostly revolve around memorization of crucial concepts.  
<https://arcprize.org/blog/arc-agi-3-gpt-5-5-opus-4-7-analysis>  
Another new benchmark where frontier models solved 0% is this one:   
‘Given only a compiled binary and its documentation, agents must architect and implement a complete codebase that reproduces the original program's behavior.’  
<https://programbench.com/>

## Politics, Society & Safety

Lawmakers were shown how jailbroken AIs can be used for terrorism quite easily, which  
is a big problem and very hard to fix.  
‘House Homeland Security Chair Andrew Garbarino (R-N.Y.) told reporters after the presentation that he asked one large language model how to kidnap a member of Congress. “It spit out an answer in under three seconds. \[It offered\] ways to find them, where to look for them. You know, the best spots to do it,” he said. ‘  
<https://www.politico.com/news/2026/04/22/ai-chatbots-jailbreak-safety-00887869>  
  
China blocked the acquisition of Manus by Meta, which was Meta’s attempt to catch up in the agentic coding game. This could be another indicator of AI competition heating up on nation level.   
<https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj0v0gr2yz7o>  
  
Also, it seems that China is already rolling out humanoid robots to help sort packages for   
logistic companies.  
‘In several of these centers, the embodied AI robots have already reached over 85% of human-level efficiency while operating stably 24/7. The company is set to begin batch deliveries of robots at the thousand-unit scale in Q2 this year.’  
<https://x.com/CyberRobooo/status/2049118436870259192>  
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m9RBRUyQAcc>  
  
Bloomberg also just released a piece about humanoid robots trying to distinguish between   
real impact and overhyped promises.  
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UQZooauU-FQ>  
  
It seems that over 30% of all websites of 2025 are now AI-generated, whereas that number was 0 just 3 years ago. This fuels the dead internet theory, where most interactions online  
will be between AIs or humans and AIs, and fewer and fewer human-to-human engagements.  
<https://x.com/AISafetyMemes/status/2049195355305369761>  
<https://www.404media.co/study-finds-a-third-of-new-websites-are-ai-generated/>  
  
The co-founder of Anthropic thinks automated AI research might happen by 2028.  
This is a reason why I find courses on AI safety and existential risk very important and would encourage you to ask for these when evaluations of your study program happen.  
A lot of safety researchers think that automated AI research will lead to an intelligence explosion that will make the containment and steering of AI models impossible, as they   
will be vastly smarter than the humans trying to control them, leaving us at their mercy.  
<https://x.com/jackclarkSF/status/2051312759594471886>  
  
Anthropic also supports research where they let a model introspect and self-report  
problematic behaviours that it might have picked up during training.   
<https://alignment.anthropic.com/2026/introspection-adapters/>  
  
Here is a very interesting interview with one of the few researchers who works on AI-welfare and AI consciousness.  
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e5plaO-ziEs>  
  
I also saw a lot of media about AI in military use cases, and I just want you to practice critical engagement with such media, as a lot of it contains state, military, and tech propaganda.   
Ask yourself questions like:   
Were ethical concerns addressed, and to what degree?   
Did they have an expert for ethics or only militarists and tech people?  
Were critical questions asked at all, and how were they answered?  
Are statements substantiated with adequate arguments, and were counterarguments provided, and addressed?
