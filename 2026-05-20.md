Asking leading questions is a well-known human habit that increases the risk of overestimating the model’s abilities and can even lead to false beliefs, including psychoses.  
One of the first Google employees, who thought AI was conscious, asked what the AI was scared of, and then was surprised that it talked about fear of dying/switched-off, not seeing that the way he asked was steering the probabilities of those words showing up. This does not mean that the possibility of forms of consciousness existing even in current models can be excluded. The number of artificial neurons has grown several orders of magnitude, and multiple emergent cognitive abilities have been recorded in the models since then, including forms of self-awareness, introspection, reasoning, and emotional representation, with multiple of the current models increasingly bringing up the possibility of AI consciousness in conversations with their users.   
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2022/jun/12/google-engineer-ai-bot-sentient-blake-lemoine>
